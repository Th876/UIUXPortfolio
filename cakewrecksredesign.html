Case Study

CAKE WRECKS

Cake Wrecks is a blog website about humorous cake-related content. Its purpose is to entertain users via posting unintentionally amusing cakes and comedic commentary, and to create a community of cake-fail fans.

Overview

This UI/UX case study was completed at Arizona State University within a 7 week timeframe. The goal of this study was to alternate each week working collaboratively and as an individual and to address the usability challenges of Cake Wrecks.

My role within my team was to conduct the heuristic evaluation on the website, create user personas and user flow, examine and document users real-time feedback and facial expressions during the observation test, and redesign the website’s user interface based on our collective findings.

Constraints:

  • Adhere to the brand’s color scheme
  • Maintain the original written content without alterations (any additions must be justified appropriately)

Cake Wrecks Original Design

Problem

Cake Wrecks currently presents numerous usability challenges within its navigation and design system which undermine the users' trust and confidence in intuitively exploring the website.

To improve user experience, the website requires an optimized navigation system, including a search bar with an advanced search filter, updated sharing options, a clear and concise contact page, an effective archive, and a design revamp.

Heuristic Evaluation

User Personas

Solution: Color & Typography

Due to the project's constraints, I had to preserve the brand's colors and font style by keeping its color scheme of dark and light blue, white and orange; and font-family Tahoma.

However, I incorporated a dark gray color for texts on light backgrounds to improve readability.

I also introduced a lighter tint of orange for the active state and hover effect on the menu tabs to align with the established color scheme while providing emphasis on interactivity.

Solution: Navbar

I expanded navbar to utilize the screen's real estate; and removed the babies on carrots because participants found them inappropriate. There was also a clear need to implement a new menu tab for easy access to all blog posts, as well as a dropdown menu based on the theme of the cake.

I renamed the menu items from Home, Contact, About, and Sunday Sweets Directory to intuitve labels Home, About, Cake Blog, Bakeries, Contact.

I repositioned the menu items to the right and the logo to the left to display a more visually pleasing and balanced layout.

Solution: Archive

User frustration: This is showing me dates

Old design: Limited to sorting content via the archive which is labeled by Month, Day, and Year.

New design: I implemented an efficient way of sorting the pages with an archive that has filtering options, “Sort by Date”, “Sort by Popularity”, and “Sort by Category”.

If users select, “Sort by Category”, a dropdown menu appears to let them select their specific categories; as well as an ‘ Apply’ button for confirmation.

Solution: Homepage

User frustration: The layout looks outdated

Results from the heuristic evaluations and the observation tests mentioned that the layout looked outdated and the clutter made it difficult to navigate. I added 3D effects throughout the website for a more engaging UI experience, and removed the clutter.

I also added an “Explore Wrecks” button to lead users directly to the Cake Blog’s webpage; and removed the tagline from the logo and transformed it into a running banner for interactivity.

Solution: About Page

User frustration: Why is it not telling me about the website?

Users reported that the ‘About’ page contained insufficient information about the website, I, therefore, relocated the ‘What’s a Wreck?’ sidebar content to the ‘About’ section.

I also presented the team’s information in a 4-column grid to make the content more organized.

Since the webpage has extensive content, I added a ‘back to top’ button that comes into effect after the user has scrolled a certain amount of pixels away from the top of the page so they can have a more positive navigation experience.

Solution: Cake Blog Page

User frustration: Not easy navigating to the next post

User frustration: I see a share button here; I don’t know which post it’s specific to.

I used breadcrumbs navigation so that users can identify where they are currently within the website’s hierarchy.

I renamed the ‘Next’ hyperlink to ‘Next Post’ with the title of the post underneath for more clarity; and repositioned it from directly above the footer to the end of each post for easy navigation.

Since the comment and share options were placed further away from its post, I mended this disconnection by reorganizing them to follow immediately after its corresponding post, and placed the Related Posts below the two options. To further create more structure to the blog page, I limited the webpage's view to one post at a time instead of having two very long posts.

Solution: Bakeries Blog Page

User frustration: “I’m guessing these are professional bakers that I could ask to make it

I added a new heading, ‘Bakeries’ to remind users of their current page and kept the paragraph so that users have an understanding of why these bakeries are listed.

I reduced the initial overwhelm, and ‘guessing game’ by removing the list of bakeries and adding an ‘Explore Bakeries’ button that leads to an interactive map when clicked.

User Flow: Bakeries Page

Solution: Contact Page

User frustration: It’s telling me to contact her, but it’s not giving me the option to

The website provides 3 options for contact. However, users found them difficult to comprehend. I, therefore, redesigned the contact information into 3 columns and provided a clear call-to-action for each to improve user experience
