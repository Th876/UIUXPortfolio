Outdated Design Wrecks
User Experience

For over ten years, Cake Wrecks has delighted fans with hilarious baking fails from amateurs and pros alike. But as the blog aged, its outdated design and clunky navigation complicated the user experience.


So, we asked: How might we transform Cake Wrecks' interface to enhance user experience and simplify navigation?

Whipping Up a Solution

We began by pinpointing usability issues and gathering user feedback. From these insights, I set out to redesign the website from scratch. My goal? A modern interface with enhanced navigation that makes it easy for users to explore, share and inquire about cake fails on any device.

Context

This was a collaborative project at Arizona State University. We followed a blended design process—Double Diamond for problem-solving and Design Thinking elements to ensure user empathy and identify pain points.

Slicing through the Wreck

To understand user frustration, we conducted a heuristic evaluation on the old Cake Wrecks website. This exploration revealed how users found and interacted with blog posts and guided our individual efforts to redesign the user interface and address the issues.

Sifting the Audience

We kicked off our research with a screener survey to filter our target users, i.e., those who engaged with cake fails and baking communities, by asking questions about demographics, interests, and browsing habits.

Wreck-ognizing Patterns Round 1

Based on insights from our 20 screener survey respondents, we selected 6 participants for a moderated observation test on the old Cake Wrecks website. This test involved real-time scenarios and tasks, revealing how users navigated the site, where they stumbled, and what made them smile (or frown).

Before jumping into the tasks, participants filled out a brief pre-test questionnaire to capture their initial expectations. After the test, a post-test questionnaire provided insight into their final thoughts.

Wreck-ognizing Patterns Round 2

I conducted a second observation test with the same six participants. Results showed that while users generally loved the redesign, opinions on the new interactive map feature were mixed.

Participants’ Feedback on the Interactive Map

Positive 👍🏼

  • “The new map is fantastic! I love how it shows all the bakeries at once in such an interactive way. It’s so much easier to find what I’m looking for.”
  • “The map really adds a fun element to finding bakeries. Loving this new feature.”
  • “The map's a feature I didn’t know I needed! Searching doesn't feel so tedious anymore.”

Mixed 😐

  • “The map is cool, but I still prefer a good old list. If the old design’s list was just a bit cleaner and more organized, it would be perfect.”
  • “I like the map for browsing, but for quick access, a simpler list would be more my speed—especially if it were less cluttered.”
  • “The map is appealing, but sometimes I just want a simple list. If the list view were more organized, I’d probably use that instead.”

Some users enjoyed the map's dynamic card format for displaying bakeries, while others preferred a straightforward list. This feedback indicated that the map’s pop-up feature may not suit everyone, which highlights the need for improvement in how I present bakery information.

Refining the Recipe

To address the mixed feedback, I returned to the drawing board and created a more organized list for finding bakeries intuitively and efficiently.

Next, I conducted A/B testing with six new participants from the screener survey pool who were interested in cake fails. Their task was to find a bakery in Atlanta, Georgia, and share their thoughts on the design.

Interestingly, results showed an even split between the interactive map and the list. This insight led to my final redesign
decision—a toggle feature that lets users switch between the embedded map view and the list view.

Here's how each option of the toggle feature serves different user needs:

  • Interactive Map View: A modern, engaging way for users to discover bakeries and enhance their browsing experience while addressing frustration with outdated features.
  • List View: A structured, straightforward option for finding specific bakeries that caters to users who prefer a traditional, organized navigation approach.

Takeaways

This redesign has transformed how users interact with the site, as they’ve reported it’s easier to navigate and more engaging. While the final design is more inclusive and efficient, it also paves the way for future growth—a win-win for everyone!

This project has been a valuable learning experience. Here are some key takeaways:

  • The Power of Iterative Design: Running multiple observation and A/B tests showed me how refining designs based on user feedback enhances usability.
  • Interpreting A/B Test Results: A/B testing can reveal complex user preferences. Combining elements from different versions is often more effective than choosing just one.
  • Importance of Accessibility: Accessibility goes beyond compliance. A floating accessibility icon with a hide option emphasizes the importance of creating an equitable experience for all users.
  • Designing for Simplicity: This redesign reinforced that simplicity is key. Finding the balance between functionality and ease of use creates a more enjoyable user experience.
